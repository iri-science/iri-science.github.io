ALS, APS, NSLS-II

The Light Sources Pathfinder is focused on implementing a common prompt processing pipeline for beamlines at each of three light source facilities to each of three High Performance Computing (HPC) facilities. The light sources are the Advanced Light Source (ALS) at Lawrence Berkeley National Laboratory (LBNL), the Advanced Photon Source (APS) at Argonne National Laboratory (ANL), and the National Synchrotron Light Source 2 (NSLS-II) at Brookhaven National Laboratory (BNL). The computing facilities are the Argonne Leadership Computing Facility (ALCF), the National Energy Research Scientific Computing Center (NERSC), and the Oak Ridge Leadership Computing Facility (OLCF).

Initial efforts are focused on three tomography beamlines, one at each participating light source. Once that is up and running, the pipeline will be expanded to include three ptychography beamlines, one at each participating light source. The intent is to invest the efforts of multiple facilities in an integrated approach to making remote HPC easily available to experiments at all DOE light sources.
