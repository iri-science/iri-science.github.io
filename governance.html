IRI Governance

One of the main activities in FY24 is to develop the program governance structure. This includes three clear areas:

  • Federal oversight. This includes the IRI Executive Authority and IRI Steering Committee (led by ASCR Facilities Division), and will include mechanisms for accountability to other contributing offices.
  • Management Council. This includes the management structures (defined in more detail below), and the Technical Subcommittees who will execute the work required. Initially, this will consist primarily of ASCR staff, but will expand as funding becomes available for IRI activities.
  • User groups. This includes computing facilities, science teams.

Governance structure will be inclusive; other offices will be engaged and participate.

IRI Executive Subcommittee

The IRI Executive Committee is composed of ASCR facility directors. Their primary responsibilities include:

  • Scope Definition: Define the overall scope of the project based on input from the DOE SC IRI Steering Committee, ensuring that the objectives are clear, achievable, and aligned with IRI goals and facility missions and goals.
  • Approach Prioritization: Prioritize the activities and approaches recommended by the leadership group, determining which strategies will best achieve the IRI objectives efficiently and effectively, given facility mission and resources.
  • Resource Allocation: Allocate resources (people, technical) strategically, ensuring the IRI program has what it needs to succeed while efficiently using organizational assets. Certain resource allocation decisions will depend on DOE acceptance.
  • Ongoing Oversight: Maintain ongoing oversight of the IRI effort, monitoring progress and ensuring it remains aligned with its defined scope and objectives.
  • Annual Reviews: Conduct yearly reviews of the IRI progress, assessing achievements, challenges, and areas for improvement. Provide constructive feedback and adjustments to strategies as necessary in collaboration with the DOE SC IRI Steering Committee.
  • Risk Management: Identify potential risks and challenges of the effort, developing and implementing strategies to mitigate these risks effectively.
  • Communication and Coordination: Facilitate open communication and coordination between the IRI management council, stakeholders, and other relevant parties, ensuring that all are aligned and informed.

IRI Leadership Group

The IRI Leadership Group consists of representatives, one from each of the 5 ASCR facilities. The IRI Leadership Group is a conduit for communication of objectives and progress: it communicates with the leads of the technical and engagement/outreach subcommittees and also reports into the IRI Executive committee.

Technical Subcommittees

Outreach and Engagement

The role of the Outreach/Engagement Technical subcommittee is to coordinate work being done with the pathfinder science teams, identify common requirements and communicate to the Leadership team. This subcommittee is also responsible for broader community engagement, working with science teams who wish to engage more closely with IRI and bringing them into the IRI framework.

Over time, the set of active IRI projects will expand as resources become available. The list of initial Pathfinder projects is an initial list of projects, not a complete list of projects - facilities and programs that have an interest in working in this space should work with the Engagement subcommittee on establishing collaboration contact points, readiness levels, requirements, and so forth.

Interfaces

This IRI Interfaces Technical Subcommittee works to gather requirements, define use cases, and specify unified interfaces for the DOE ASCR Facilities. Beyond defining Application Programmable Interfaces (API), this may include the integration of front end applications (e.g. jupyter notebooks) and orchestration systems (including hosted orchestration tools).

This group is tasked with enhancing the “user experience” for interaction and management of complex workflows across High-Performance Computing Facilities, High-Performance Data Facilities, and High-Performance Scientific Network Facilities, thereby facilitating a more cohesive and efficient research infrastructure. The group will consider applicable standards and build upon the work of other organizations where available to deliver a reference IRI interface implementation.

Trusted IRI Designs (TRUSTID)

The Integrated Research Infrastructure (IRI) TRUSTID Technical Subcommittee is established to enable trustworthy and performant execution of cross facility workflows. The TRUSTID subcommittee’s mission is to create a set of design patterns and policy elements that mitigate risks, safeguard assets, and enable a trusted environment for transformative open science.

Cybersecurity enables open science when security architectures and technologies are effectively integrated into performant and functional workflow designs from the beginning. It is essential that security teams and other stakeholders of all sites and facilities engaged in IRI build partnerships to develop trusted multi-facility workflows that are acceptable to all IRI participants. It is an explicit goal of the IRI TRUSTID Technical Subcommittee to create the design patterns and collaborative relationships to bring this about.
